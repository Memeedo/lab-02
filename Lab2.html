Information security in the tech world has a rich history marked by significant milestones; for instance, the term "computer virus" was coined in the early 1980s, and one of the first known viruses, "Elk Cloner," infected Apple II computers. In 1995, the first known phishing attack occurred, targeting eBay users, and since then, phishing has evolved into a major cybersecurity threat. The introduction of the Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS) in 2004 aimed to protect credit card information, while the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), enacted in 2018, set new standards for data protection and privacy in the EU. Ransomware attacks have surged in recent years, with high-profile cases like the Colonial Pipeline attack in 2021 highlighting vulnerabilities in critical infrastructure. Additionally, the rise of cloud computing has introduced new security challenges, making data breaches more prevalent; in fact, the 2020 Verizon Data Breach Investigations Report indicated that 86% of breaches were financially motivated. Finally, the growing importance of cybersecurity skills has led to a surge in demand for professionals, with the cybersecurity job market projected to grow significantly in the coming years.
